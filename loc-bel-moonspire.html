DUNGEON MASTERS ONLY

Location: Temple of the Moonspire

Exterior Description

The Temple of the Moonspire juts like a cracked fang from the hillside, its obsidian spire reaching crookedly toward the night sky. No windows, no banners, no comforting signs of divine welcome. The main gates are shaped like a snarling maw, carved into the rockface itself, guarded by half-statues of orcish warlords locked in a death cry. The crescent ridge surrounding the temple casts perpetual shade, even in daylight. Strange runes—neither wholly celestial nor infernal—glow faintly along the spire at night. The air carries the iron tang of old blood and damp moss. Once a holy place to the Indigenous Orc Tribes who followed Astaroth the Terrible, it was desecrated by early human settlers. Now it is haunted by lingering spiritual unrest.

Interior Description

  • The Hollow Choir Hall: A great rotunda filled with broken pews, bone wind chimes, and murals of celestial battles between the Archon Astaroth and "the Serpent Above." Pillars carved with scenes of orcish ancestors bowing to a moon-eyed archon. Whispered sounds echo disturbingly, drawing Echo Wights if voices rise too loud.
  • Chamber of the Lunar Brand: A sacrificial altar surrounded by black-stone braziers. A hanging chain ends in a crescent-shaped brand. Touching the altar and passing a DC 18 Wisdom save grants a vision of Astaroth. Hidden below is an urn containing an orcish prophecy scroll.
  • The Spire Ascent: Vertical climb through decaying stairs and rope bridges. Moonstone relics embedded in walls glow for orc-blooded characters. Traps include collapsing planks, dart runes, banshee ward. Halfway up is a skeleton holding a sealed, humming clay orb.
  • The Eye of Astaroth: A cracked chamber at the peak. A star-map and dais remain. At midnight, moonlight activates the dais. Speaking "Astaroth" may summon a ghostly presence to orc-blooded characters, offering cryptic aid... or a curse.

Backstory

In life, Krogar Vexgrim was the last Grand Warden of the Moonspire Temple. He died defending its sacred rites after the human settlers attacked. Mortally wounded, he cursed the desecrators and died at the altar. Now, as a ghostly guardian, he judges intruders based on their purpose, waiting for a worthy heir to restore the temple.
